34 - When the sun is on the side, the shadows on the ground must all be parallel. Can you explain why in this NASA photo the shadow of the LEM and those of the stones in the foreground are clearly divergent from each other?

Let's start immediately with the first serious inaccuracy stated by the documentary, and that is this: "When the sun is on the side, the shadows on the ground must all be parallel. Absolutely wrong and misleading statement. In a photo, as in a perspective drawing, the shadows of objects will appear parallel in a completely exceptional situation, that is, when the very distant light source, (as is the Sun, but also the Moon for lunar shadows) is at 90 degrees with respect to the photographer's lens. In all other cases, where the Sun is not perfectly at 90°, the shadows on the ground, parallel in reality, by the laws of perspective will converge towards a point on the horizon called the "vanishing point" located on the optical horizon of the image. Here we put three photos with three very clear and unequivocal examples. As we can see in all three images, the Sun is on the side, but only in the third image the shadows are all horizontal and parallel. This immediately gives us an easy rule to understand when the Sun is at 90°: as we see from the examples proposed with the Sun from the side and incident, all shadows approaching the horizon will tend to become horizontal, but only when the Sun is at 90° with respect to the photographer, even the nearest shadow will be horizontal. In other cases, the shadows near each other will be tilted. Be careful, the observation that the documentary makes, and also repeated by the author in later moments, namely that if in a photo the farthest shadow is horizontal, then the sun is at 90°, is a serious conceptual error. As we see from the three previous examples, the shadows on the horizon all become horizontal, even if the sun is not at 90°, on the contrary it will be the closest shadow to determine the position of the sun, if this is horizontal, the sun will be to the side and at 90° with respect to the photographer's lens, if it is not horizontal, the sun will be to the side, but not at 90°. Here is an enlargement of one of the three photos that demonstrates it unequivocally. For this reason, we remind the author of the documentary and the consulting photographers of a rule that they seem to have ignored, namely that divergent shadows can be produced not only by a lighthouse, but also by the sun if it is on the side but not perfectly at 90°. In the NASA image disputed by the documentary, could the Sun be at 90°? link In the photo AS14-68-9487 we can absolutely exclude that the sun is at 90°, as not only is the nearest shadow tilted, but since we are looking at the original photo (downloaded from the Apollo Lunar Surface Journal and without cleaning it up as the documentary did), we notice that a lens flare effect is perfectly visible and at the top left there is a rounded shadow produced by the lens hood on the lens; which excludes beyond any reasonable doubt the fact that the light source was at 90°, since, in that case, the Sun would not have cast the shadow of the lens hood on the lens and would not even have produced the lens flare effect. To produce that shadow and the aberration effect on luminance, the rays had to be necessarily incident on the lens so the source could not be at 90°. That said, the NASA photo does not present any anomaly in the arrangement of the shadows, and we can also see that the same shadows are positioned in tune with the image of the colonnade that we observed above and we demonstrate it with this additional image. For this reason, question 34, containing a serious conceptual error, has no reason to be asked and in the next answers we will identify the exact position of the sun in the image, correctly applying the theory of shadows.

35 - Since this scene should be illuminated by the sun, which is millions of kilometers away, can you explain why the shadows in the photo lead back to a light source that is not far from the left edge of the frame?

We add the photo attached to the application. In this image a disconcerting mistake is made, namely that we would like to determine the position of the light source by extending on the ground the lines that start from the shadows and converge at a point, lines marked in yellow in the image. Apart from the fact that those lines are really drawn in a surreal way, in particular the line that should follow the direction of the shadow of the LEM, a line moved from the centerline of the shadow itself, so created only to find a non-existent point of convergence where you wanted it to be found. But the conceptual error is even more serious. As we have already explained, the extension of the direction of the parallel shadows on the ground will give us the vanishing point of parallel lines on the optical horizon and not the position of the light source. That vanishing point will instead mark the orthogonal projection of the light source on the horizon, as the shadow theory explicitly predicts. Below we put an explanatory image taken from a perspective drawing manual with an example of shadow study and we see that the red lines that extend the profile of the shadows meet on the optical horizon and determine the orthogonal projection of the light source. To geometrically determine the position of the light source we must join with lines the extreme edges of the shadow with the parts of the object that determine the shadows of the same extreme points and which in the example shown are the corners of the parallelepiped. If the lines are drawn correctly (in the example they are the most marked black lines) these lines will meet at a point (s) which will be the source of light and must be orthogonal to the vanishing point generated by the convergence of the direction of the shadows on the optical horizon (S1). Below we put a real image where the shadow study has been applied and where you can see the position of the sun with the vanishing point and shadow tracking indicated. Now, before correctly applying the theory of shadows to the NASA image, let's observe one thing. In the original image, not subtly cleaned up in the documentary, it is possible to glimpse in the lens flare effect produced by the Sun on the lens, light rays that will mark the direction of the Sun's rays. Below is the link to the original NASA image. We see them even better in this image where we have highlighted them by changing the contrast of the image a bit, but they are still visible even in the original image above. These rays, if they had not been erased by the cutting and cleaning of the image made by the documentary, would have helped to identify the direction of the Sun. It would have been enough to extend them to their meeting point. Now let's correctly apply the theory of shadows to the NASA image, using, in addition to shadows, also the direction of the rays in the photo: we will obtain the exact position of the light source in the photo by establishing the optical horizon and the vanishing point perpendicular to the position of the Sun. As we can see, the position of the Sun is perfectly compatible with the shadow produced by the lens hood and with the direction of the sun's rays already present in the image, but not only that, the horizon line identified is very close to the central crosses of the camera's reseau plate, crosses that should indicate the optical horizon in case the camera was fixed on a tripod and with the lens perfectly parallel to the ground. Since the astronaut who took the photo was not a tripod, the one obtained is a really good coincidence. As we see the documentary with his fashion photographer consultants, not knowing how to correctly apply the theory of shadows, they were unable to establish the exact position of the sun.

It is legitimate to ask: why was the perspective and theory of shadows not evaluated by an expert in the field, for example an architect or a technical draughtsman who with a pencil and a ruler, pulling a few lines, would have quickly identified the position of the sun? Unfortunately, even at this juncture, the carelessness of the documentary proved to be decisive and functional only to a conspiracy narrative. After this unedifying figure, it is better for the photographic consultants to express themselves on issues related to their professionalism (e.g. how to highlight the beauty of the models, or how to promote a commercial product), but leave the theory of shadows to those who have studied it, and we say this in their interest, precisely to avoid bad figures like these. Unfortunately, in the image shown to the consultants, the sky was cut off, partly hiding the shadow of the lens hood and partly also the sun's rays, and even the lens flare effect, clearly visible in the black sky, was attenuated, so the photographers were not shown an original image, but a cropped image, and this distorted their judgment. While as we can see in the original image of the Apollo Lunar Surface Journal that we show below, (including links), you can see a strong lens flare effect and in the effect you can see the sun's rays very well. In conclusion: the cropped image did not allow photographers to correctly assess the position of the sun. In fact, being able to observe the lens flare effect very evident in the black of the sky (which was partially cut off in the photo), plus the rounded shadow of the lens hood and the sun's rays that alone would have indicated the position of the sun, (even those partially clipped), all this would probably have changed their evaluation. The fact is that the position of the sun in the photo is perfectly deducible and is certainly not where the photographers imagined it using spans instead of pencil, ruler and perspective rules.

37 - Since the sun, being millions of kilometers away, must cast sharp shadows on the ground, can you explain why in these photos, all around the astronaut's shadow, there is a strongly blurred outline?

To say that: the Sun, being millions of miles away, must cast sharp shadows on the ground, is a nonsense that even the most amateur of photographers could disprove in a few photos. Despite this, some self-styled photographers aligned with the lunar conspiracy theory have tried in every way to prove that the statement was true, and that the sun's shadows fade on Earth only because there is an atmosphere. Just to get this objection out of the way, let's put this photo of the ISS, with a very blurred shadow and with the Earth seen below, so that the clouds do not project diffused light on the solar panels, and with this example we show that even in a vacuum penumbra forms in the solar shadows. https://lc.cx/hOPyHQ Now we will not waste time explaining the phenomenon of penumbra illustrated in detail in at least 3 dedicated videos of which we put the links below: Youtube Youtube Youtube rather we will try to understand why the shadow in the image chosen by the documentary is so blurred. The image (AS12-47-6896) below is taken from the Apollo 12 mission, which was the mission that carried out its extravehicular activities with the Sun lower, between 8 and 17 degrees above the horizon. The shadows in that case were very long and this accentuated the half-light. To realize we must have another image in which the shadows are seen horizontally. In the photo AS12-46-6818, below, taken during the same mission, as we can see the shadow is exactly 3.3 times the height of the astronaut. In addition, the offending shadow in photo AS12-47-6896 is visibly projected into a depression in the ground and this accentuates even more the length of the shadow and the penumbra that surrounds it. This explains the exceptional nature of the half-light that has made photographers suspicious. Their reaction would have been very different if they had seen the photos of Apollo 16, where the third extravehicular activity was conducted with the Sun at almost 49 degrees above the horizon and in fact the shadows are very little blurred as we see in this further photo AS16-110-18022. This difference is due to the fact that in Apollo 16 the shadow is much less elongated and therefore sharper. As you may have noticed, in the image AS12-47-6896 we have added the vanishing lines, just to show that the 4 shadows we see, net of the irregularities of the terrain, converge at a point on the optical horizon. This guarantees us that no lamp behind the astronaut (even more so if close) could ever have made the shadows converge towards a perspective vanishing point on the horizon, which on the contrary guarantees us the substantial parallelism of those shadows, net of the irregularities of the terrain and the perfect verticality of the subjects portrayed. After these explanations we can say that the shade of the shadow in photo AS12-47-6896 is absolutely normal and determined by the low solar inclination and the consequent length of the shadow (three times the height of the astronaut) also confirmed by this other photo of the same roll where we see the shadow of the flag in the exceptionally long ground, a context condition that has accentuated the formation of penumbra. In conclusion: the documentary showed a total ignorance on this subject, moreover there was a lack of analysis of the context in which the photo proposed to the photographers had been taken (with low sun and exceptionally long shadows) and this produced the most misinformation page of the whole documentary, further invalidating the credibility of the photographic consultants regarding technical and specific topics related to optics and shadow theory, Topics that should be studied at the table and certainly not improvised by eye.
