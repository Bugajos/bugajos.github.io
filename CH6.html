Question 17 - Since there is no humidity on the moon, and that the solar wind dissipates any electrostatic charges almost instantaneously, can you explain why dust remains constantly attached to all kinds of materials, from astronauts' suits to cameras, from rover surfaces to the glass of camera lenses?

The question contains completely incorrect information, namely that "the solar wind dissipates any electrostatic charges almost instantaneously". In fact, the document, which the same question shows with an underline, does not refer to the electrostatic effect but to the triboelectric one. Below we see the NASA page from which the erroneous information that accompanies the question was extracted. link What is the difference between the electrostatic and triboelectric phenomena? Although both phenomena arise from the same cause (the surface electric charge that accumulates on insulating objects by rubbing), the electrostatic effect is limited to causing repulsion or attraction between electrostatically charged objects, the triboelectric effect instead refers to the generation of particularly high surface electric charges capable of generating sparks that we perceive as electric shocks, e.g. when we touch metal objects or other people. We now know from direct experience that these effects also exist on Earth and we know that the electrostatic effect is present in any atmospheric and air humidity condition, while the triboelectric effect occurs only in very particular conditions where the air humidity is very low. On the Moon, the electrostatic effect produced several drawbacks, as dust stuck everywhere, starting with the lenses of the cameras, film cameras and cameras, as well as of course the suits through which the dust entered the LEM and was breathed in by the astronauts. These inconveniences have also caused allergic rhinitis to some of them, but never real dangers. The triboelectric effect, on the other hand, with its sparks, could have created problems for the rover's electronic circuits or the PLSS, the survival system in the astronauts' backpack, so the phenomenon could be potentially dangerous for the survival of the astronauts themselves, as well as their electronic equipment. The NASA document (to which the documentary mistakenly refers) states two important things, namely that on the Moon there is a double boundary condition: during the day the presence of the solar wind composed of a flow of electrons from the sun would have prevented the accumulation on the insulating surfaces of charges of thousands of volts capable of generating sparks (triboelectric effect), as the electrons of the solar wind are themselves conductive and prevent excessive voltage accumulation. On the contrary, at night, the poor ability of the lunar exosphere to discharge accumulated currents would have created a danger with respect to the possible generation of sparks, in particular during the use of the lunar rover which produced intense rubbing phenomena on the regolith. However, since the missions all took place during the day and under the sun, the problem did not arise. The same document, however, also speaks of the electrostatic effect and does not say at all that this is not present, on the contrary it analyzes its characteristics. A page of the same document (below) reports the presence of suspended dust due not only to micro meteorite impacts, but also to electrostatic charges that cause suspension up to one meter in height of electrostatically charged dust that moves on the lunar surface, in particular in the vicinity of the terminator, Which by the way (the terminator) was precisely the climatically favorable zone, where all six moon landings took place and this perfectly explains both the presence of electrostatic phenomena during extravehicular activities, as well as the absence of dangerous triboelectric phenomena. link In practice, the author of American Moon only had to read the entire NASA document which he himself underlined, to answer himself regarding question 17. However, given the ignorance shown by the author, I would advise him to study these documents: Electrostatic charging of the lunar surface, The Effects of Lunar Dust on EVA Systems During the Apollo Missions, Investigations of electrostatic dust transport on the lunar surface.

Question 18 - Can you explain how layers like these, which definitely look like mud, can form on the wheels of the rover?

Once it has been established that important electrostatic forces act on the Moon, thinking that particles of dust can be deposited and adhere to surfaces is completely obvious. On the other hand, to say that those deposits in the photo resemble mud has no logical reason and even less a visual similarity. In the first place, the mud has a very different color, due to iron oxides, which are only possible in the presence of atmospheric oxygen, but if those regolith deposits had really been gray mud they would have first of all clogged the links of the wheels of the lunar rover, which were built from a dense grid and tight metal meshes. In fact, if the mud manages to adhere to the smooth lateral surface of the rubber tire, as we see in the figure attached to the question, imagine if it had not filled the porous grid of the rover's wheels, filling the inside and outside of the wheel. On the other hand, we do not see any deposits on the metal grid of which the wheel is made, and not even deposits of the supposed mud can be seen inside the grid, a sign that there was no mud, but only perfectly dry dust, that is, regolith that had flowed from the grid without remaining inside. For this reason, the limited deposits of regolith on the wheel rim are due to gravitational reasons (the most consistent deposits at the bottom) and electrostatic reasons (the sparse ones on the walls).

19 - Can you explain how the lunar dust manages to remain so compact, even preserving the shape of the numbers after it has been removed from the place where it was formed?

First let's explain what the experiment shown was for, since American Moon didn't even mention it. In the figure below you can see a metal plate with plugs of reflective material embedded in it. This plate is part of an Apollo 14 experiment and had to be covered with regolith that was poured with a certain force by the astronauts to make it adhere to the inclined plate and "thus be able to evaluate the effect produced by the lunar dust on the optical properties (absorption and emissivity) of 12 different thermal coatings". Here is an in-depth look at the experiment: In that document we find this observation: Although dust adhered readily to the astronauts' suits, it could be brushed off easily, except for that part that had been "rubbed in" the fabric. Similarly, dust sprinkled onto the thermal degradation sample (TDS) array was easily brushed off, although that portion of the dust that filled in the number depressions of the TDS tended to cohere, as may be seen in figure 4-7. In this case, the dust has been formed into the pattern of the numbers shown; then, when the TDS was tapped, the dust remained intact and bounced out of the depression Although the dust adhered easily to the astronauts' suits, it could be brushed off easily, except for that part that had been "rubbed" on the fabric. Similarly, the dust scattered over the thermal degradation sample (TDS) matrix was easily brushed off, although that part of the dust that filled the numerical depressions of the TDS tended to remain cohesive, as can be seen in figure 4-7. In this case, the dust has been formed in the pattern of the numbers shown; then, when the TDS was touched, the dust remained intact and bounced out of the depression. As we can see, the adhesive effect of lunar dust had not escaped scientists. However, if we look more closely at the clean plate, we see that the numbers were carved into the metal and this certainly favored the cohesion of the regolith, as the dust was trapped and compressed in a kind of mold by the astronauts who launched it, from which it then came out partially maintaining its shape. But if the regolith wasn't wet, how could it stay so cohesive? For three reasons: one mechanical, regolith has never undergone erosion / smoothing phenomena produced by atmospheric agents. Coming from micro meteorite impacts, regolith has extremely small and jagged grains, which can easily get stuck together during compression. The second reason is the electrostatic attraction favored by the total lack of moisture, and the third reason is the Van der Walls forces. These adhesive forces, the same ones that allow insects to walk on glass or geckos to hang from ceilings, are formed when structures of a few microns (such as the hairs of the legs of flies, geckos or precisely the pointed appendages of regolith grains) encounter other materials. The almost molecular size of those structures allows the molecular dipoles of the materials to interact and come into contact with each other, establishing an attraction that can not only support an insect, but even a small reptile. In any case, the property of the regolith pressed so that it maintains its shapes is confirmed in the Chinese lunar photos (footprints of the Yutu rover), Soviet (footprints of the Lunokhod rover) and American, as in the three attached figures. Question 20 - Since the Mytbusters replicated the lunar conditions, with the regolith simulant in a vacuum chamber, can you explain why they were not able to replicate the same footprints left by the astronauts in the original photos?

The answer is simple and interesting: an regolith simulant will generate an impression simulant, certainly not the same impression. One concept must be clarified: lunar regolith is produced by micro meteoric impacts at very high speed that pulverize the rocky material, vitrifying it due to the high temperatures produced by the impact, material that is then not modified by atmospheric events. On Earth there is nothing comparable as the atmosphere prevents micrometeorites from reaching the ground and all terrestrial dust is subjected to atmospheric agents. Then there are three elements that influence making it impossible for lunar footprints to replicate on Earth. First difference: as you can see from the figure, the simulant used by the MythBusters is more granular than the lunar regolith and this has already made the imprint less clear. Second difference: the iconic reference image made on the Moon is not a normal footprint, but an imprint trodden with particular force by Aldrin before being photographed. You can see it perfectly from this gif. while the MythBusters applied for their footprint a weight only equal to that of the astronaut on the Moon divided by two. In fact, other not so trodden moonprints are more similar to that of the MythBusters as we see below (AS17-146-22423) Third difference: the amount of regolith. In the MythBusters experiment, the simulant is a few centimeters thick, and underneath there is a rigid surface that prevents the shoe from sinking, while we know that on the Moon the dust is a few meters thick and this certainly tends to favor the sinking of the boot, more effectively imprinting the profile of the sole in the dust. Fourth and final difference: gravity. On the Moon, gravity is one-sixth that of the Earth, and this means that the dust grains that form the sharp corners of the lunar image are less attracted and remain in balance. On the contrary, in the Earth's footprint, the greater gravity will attract the most unbalanced grains that are those that form the sharp edges of the footprint, and this will tend to round the same edges, just as happened in the MythBusters' footprint. Taking into account these three essential elements, the satisfaction of the MythBusters in having obtained their imprint without resorting to humidity as a glue for the dust seems completely understandable, which was precisely what they wanted to demonstrate, disproving the conspiracy theory that imagined the use of humidity to keep the regolith grains cohesive. Taking into account that the MythBusters could not use real regolith, they put less pressure on the simulant, at least compared to the image of Aldrin's footprint and above all, during the experiment, having a low thickness of the simulant and a 6 times higher gravity, it really would not have been possible to achieve more. Among other things, it would have been enough to change the reference lunar footprint to minimize the differences, so the result of the MythBusters should be absolutely appreciated.

Question 23 - Since there is no atmosphere on the moon, can you explain what can slow down and sustain in mid-air the lighter particles of sand, which form clouds of dust before falling back to the ground?

This question combined with its image that we see below it is basically a provocation and deserves an equally provocative counter-question. Since dogs are notoriously not flying, can you explain what this dog in the image below sustains in the air? Leaving this crazy circle we can say with certainty that neither the dog nor the cloud taken by the wheel of the rover are supported by anything, a snapshot can be deceiving and is absolutely not suitable for describing phenomena that are essentially dynamic. In the documentary there is another simplification: it is correctly stated that the dust raised in the absence of an atmosphere will always make parabolic paths, but be careful, this does not mean that the dust particles will all make the same parabolic paths,side by side, because this is impossible, since we are dealing with the case of the wheel of a rover that raises dust on a terrain particularly bumpy. Here in the image below we see how the documentary fables about orderly parabolic paths that the moon dust should have done, which was obviously completely invented. But paradoxically, even taking that abstraction at face value, if we took a snapshot of the tail of the parabolic arch imagined by the author of the documentary, we would see that even in that case a beautiful cloud would form, as evidenced in the image below. And this is obvious, because as similar as the parabolic paths of thousands of grains of sand are, sooner or later they will differentiate and by separating their paths they will generate a cloud that can be captured by a snapshot. At this point a clear consideration arises: it is not significant that a supposed cloud appears in a static photo, what matters is that the dust, although following very different paths, falls to the ground without remaining suspended and the paths of the grains, although very differentiated, must still remain parabolic. In this GIF (which I remember is a dynamic image and not a static one) you can see unequivocally how the dust coming out of the rover's wheel behaves extremely differently in the atmosphere (left) and in the lunar vacuum (right). In the first case, you can see the air that moves the dust laterally, keeping it suspended. In the second case, the lunar one, the dust falls back and is not transported and above all does not remain suspended. In this second GIF we notice instead that the grains of dust, when they are forced to follow a forced path (in fact they all come out of the narrow space between the wheel and the wheel cover during the rover's stop) in the absence of air perfectly follow the same parabolic paths giving the idea that what comes out of the wheel is not dust, but a fabric. In conclusion: if the lunar rover had moved in the atmosphere on rough terrain and with fine dust, we would have seen the dust raised in the air much longer than we saw it on the Moon and the difference would have been the same as we see between these two images: on the left the rover in the atmosphere and on the right the rover on the Moon, and this is in tune with the behavior that a land vehicle traveling along a dusty path can cause
