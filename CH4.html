14 - Since we have examined the original videos of Spacecraft Films, and since the debunkers themselves confirm that these videos have not been edited by anyone, can you explain why in several cases there is a delay, between the question and the answer, much less than there should be if the conversation had really taken place between the earth and the moon?
Spacecraft Films DVDs are not the repositories of the historical truth of lunar missions, but are commercial products created and sold to make profits, and not to archive files, films and NASA documents. Among other things, today SCF is out of business and no longer produces its DVDs. That the SCF cannot perform this function is perfectly demonstrable. Take, for example, the Apollo 15 mission in which the supposed lack of audio delays in Earth-Moon communications were found. SCF offers a box set of 6 DVDs, this one: We know that a maximum of 240 minutes can be stored in a DVD. of audio/video content, equivalent to 4 hours. 4 hours for 6 DVDs makes 24 hours, so of that mission that lasted more than 180 hours, only a part of it was reported. It must be said that all the audio/video material coming from the mission camera and the video material coming from the lunar camera, the Maurer, has been reported in full and without cuts on the DVDs, and hence the definition of film and video unedited given by the debunkers, a wording that can be absolutely shared, but this cannot apply to the vast amount of audio that we see deposited in this storage site and shared, which NASA uses and relating only to the Apollo 15 mission archive.org For this reason it is absolutely demonstrable that the audio of the mission has been cut for about 83% in the 6 DVDs. In fact, in the site there are 48 files of which most of them last 3 hours each, the complete sum of all the audio is 141 hours and 43 minutes, and it is evident that all these hours of recorded mission could not in any way be contained in the 24 hours of the 6 SCF DVDs, hence the obvious consideration that the cut that the company made on the audio was draconian to say the least, and it is equally clear that only a website could offer all the audio of a mission, with special utilities to be able to listen to it, and certainly not video DVDs, such as SCF DVDs. Here is the initial observation demonstrated, namely that Spacecraft Films' DVDs cannot be the repositories of the historical truth of the lunar missions, because they are full of cuts on the audio. Now the question is: but was the audio with the lack of technical delays in Earth/Moon communications audio connected to the camera and that we know is "unedited" or is it audio without images, part of that 83% already cut? The chapter referred to in the DVD has the wording "audio only" and this explains everything. In fact, being a rover transfer, the audio could not be linked to the camera images, but was an audio recorded in Houston while the rover was in motion, and where for about 10 minutes the SCF superimposed the silent images of the Maurer camera taken on board the rover. As we have shown this audio not connected to the camera cannot be called unedited (it has been cut to 83%) and as we see from this image which is very explanatory, the SCF wanted to compress as much audio as possible under the part where the camera video appeared, and in fact made 15 minutes of NASA's audio fit in the only 10 minutes where the images of the camera appear from the rover, gaining 50% more information on that transfer. Then, once the black picture appeared, the SCF no longer edited the audio except by removing a few long rustles, and gaining a total of 8 minutes of duration compared to the original NASA audio. For this reason, the cuts were not made TO AVOID BOREDOM, NOT EVEN TO SAVE SPACE, BUT THE CUTS WERE MADE TO OPTIMIZE THE INFORMATION, bearing in mind that the vast majority of its buyers, having finished the images and with the advent of the black painting, would have moved on to another chapter of the mission. The reason for the audio cuts should now be clear: In order to ensure that those 10 minutes of camera video, certainly the most viewed minutes, were richer in information about the transfer to Dune Crater, SCF cut out all the downtime and hiss of the audio under the video, thus being able to offer 50% more information about that transfer. compared to the NASA audio for the same minute (i.e. about 10 min.) and this is because there was originally no synchronization between audio and video, which was present in the images coming from the camera. Not only that, but in cutting the rustling it also inadvertently cut a sentence from the Houston capcom, at minute 3:10 of the chapter, as demonstrated here a fact that unequivocally demonstrates the cuts that have taken place. Below instead we see a detail of the comparison between the audio of the SCF and that of NASA, which despite being the same audio, with the same dialogues, has very different durations: 10 against 15 minutes. The comparison between the two waveforms leaves no doubt, all the cuts (which are displayed with the lack of black parts interspersing the green ones in the waveform) are all in the first 10 minutes where the chapter proposed the images. With the advent of the black picture, cuts became very sporadic, and concerned only the longest hiss, and this was because the cuts themselves had lost their main function of condensing audio information under a video. However, given the difference of 8 minutes between the SCF audio used for this chapter and the original NASA audio that we show with this image, It is more than obvious that not only the technical delay time between the Houston question and the answer on the Moon has been cut, but also a huge amount of other hiss, which had nothing to do with technical delays, because only this choice can explain the 8 minutes difference between the durations of the two audios. It is obvious that it was the SCF that cut the rustling and not NASA that added 8 minutes of downtime. However, to close the matter definitively, let's add two images and two links. archive.org In the image above, together with the link, you can see a delay calculated in American Moon in less than a second and the corresponding page of the Apollo Journal of June 1997, so before the release of the SCF DVDs, and as we see the technical delay of 5 seconds there is. transcript In this second image with the link, you can see the page of the 1970 mission logs and where, apart from the different count of the phases of the mission (days, hours, minutes and seconds compared to only hours, minutes and seconds for the Apollo Journal), the delay is perfectly present and is still 5 seconds. The issue is now very clear and perfectly demonstrated: the cuts in the hiss, including the technical delays of Terra Luna communications, were made by the SCF for a valid and documented reason and it was possible to make them because they were audio without a video connected. In all other chapters of the DVD, where camera images are shown that are natively linked to the images, cuts to the sound with hiss would have resulted in cuts to the related filmed images, visibly breaking the sequence shot characteristic of lunar video documents and losing the DVDs' characteristic of being: FILM and VIDEO unedited. In conclusion: the debunkers were not wrong to claim that film and video were "unedited", as the films (from the camera) and the videos (from the camera with associated audio) in the DVDs are uncut. On the other hand, all the audio without association with the video from the camera as shown was MONSTROUSLY CUT by 83%, including that analyzed by American Moon with the lack of technical delays.
